Project Summary

Objective: The objective of this project is to develop a machine learning model capable of classifying tomato leaf images into two categories: Tomato_healthy and Tomato_unhealthy. The goal is to assist in early detection of diseases in tomato plants, which is critical for ensuring crop health and yield.

Methodology:

  • Dataset Preparation: The dataset was derived from the PlantVillage dataset, with images categorized by health status and various diseases. Tomato_healthy and Tomato_unhealthy categories were created for model training.
  • Image Preprocessing: Images were resized, normalized, and labels were binarized for the classification task.
  • Model Development: A Convolutional Neural Network (CNN) was built with convolutional layers, pooling layers, and dense layers for feature extraction and classification.
  • Model Evaluation: The model was evaluated using a confusion matrix, ROC curve, and precision-recall curve, showing high accuracy and strong class separation.
  • Testing: The trained model was tested on new images to predict health status, with high confidence in the results.

Results: The model achieved satisfactory accuracy, with the evaluation plots demonstrating balanced performance and a strong ability to distinguish between healthy and unhealthy leaves.

Conclusion: The developed model can assist in early detection of tomato plant diseases, helping farmers and agronomists protect their crops effectively.
